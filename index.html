AERO62520 Robotic Systems Design Project — Autonomous Rover System

Semester 2 MSc Robotics project · rover build-up · SLAM mapping · Nav2 navigation · cyber-security analysis

Goal: Develop an integrated autonomous rover capable of exploring an environment, detecting task-relevant objects, navigating towards targets, interacting with them using a robotic arm, and returning/sorting objects as part of a complete mission.

My contribution: I was mainly responsible for the rover mapping and navigation work, as well as vehicle build-up and on-robot debugging. This included preparing the Leo Rover platform, mounting and checking the main hardware, bringing up the LiDAR and base system, and testing the ROS 2 navigation pipeline on both simulation and the physical rover.

Technical evidence: My work focused on the RPLIDAR, scan filtering, SLAM Toolbox mapping, Nav2 planning/control, costmap behaviour, TF/odometry checks, RViz monitoring and frontier-exploration testing. I also helped debug practical issues such as sensor communication, frame availability, launch order, map quality and navigation parameter tuning.

18th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition

Team lead · National Second Prize · Provincial First Prize

Goal: Develop a smart vehicle capable of completing autonomous navigation and task execution in a competition environment.

My contribution: As team lead, I divided the system into perception, planning and control modules. I mainly owned the perception pipeline, including a multithreaded C++ OpenCV system and a compact SSD-MobileNet-V1 detector trained from a curated dataset. The detector signals drove speed and steering targets, helping the race car finish reliably.

17th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition — ROS Outdoor UGV

Team lead · National First Prize

Goal: Build an outdoor unmanned ground vehicle capable of mapping, localisation and autonomous waypoint navigation.

My contribution: I brought the robot up from zero in ROS, including drivers, TF frames and launch files. I integrated gmapping, AMCL and TEB, tuned the navigation stack on uneven ground, and kept parameter logs so navigation failures could be diagnosed and improved systematically.

17th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition

Perception, navigation & control · Provincial Second Prize

Goal: Develop a competition vehicle that could recognise lane/marker information and complete reliable autonomous movement under race constraints.

My contribution: I owned the software stack, built the C/C++ pipeline with OpenCV for lane/marker recognition, implemented the segment/waypoint finite-state navigation logic, and tuned closed-loop PID for speed and steering. I also added logging and parameter files so race-day changes were repeatable.
