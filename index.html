Selected Projects

18th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition

Team lead · National Second Prize · Provincial First Prize

I split the stack into perception–planning–control and owned perception. Built a multithreaded C++ OpenCV pipeline and trained a compact SSD-MobileNet-V1 from a curated dataset. The detector’s signals drive speed/steering targets, giving us a consistent race car that finished reliably.

17th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition — ROS Outdoor UGV

Team lead · National First Prize

I brought the robot up from zero in ROS: drivers, TF frames and launch files. Using gmapping + AMCL + TEB, I tuned the navigation stack until it tracked waypoints steadily on uneven ground. Biggest lesson: disciplined parameter logs make every failure explainable.

17th National Undergraduate Smart Car Competition

Perception, navigation & control · Provincial Second Prize

I owned the software stack. Built the C/C++ pipeline with OpenCV for lane/marker recognition, implemented the navigation logic (segment/waypoint FSM), and tuned closed-loop PID for speed and steering. I also added lightweight logging and parameter files so race-day changes were repeatable, which made the car steadier and much easier to debug under pressure.
